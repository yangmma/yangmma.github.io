About Me


I’m originally from Hong Kong, and currently based in Auckland, New Zealand. I first discovered my love of computer science through my high school's coding club, which introduced students to the basics of Python. As the school had no official computer science course, I instead dedicated my time to competitions, hackathons, and self-teaching, until formally learning to code at UoA.

I decided to supplement my Computer Science experience with a double major in Psychology, as I hold a keen interest on the ways human behaviour and technology can intersect. Through this, I have developed a strong interest in machine learning, and an insight into user-friendly design.

Projects

Freecycle.org Redesign

In my Human-Computer Interaction (CS345) class, I was tasked with re-designing an existing website (freecycle.org) to better accommodate usability needs. This project came in two parts: a group mobile re-design on Figma, and a personal high-fidelity web re-design.


Pay-To-Win Kettle

For the 2023 End-Of-Year Terrible Ideas Hackathon, my team and I constructed the "Pay-To-Win" 'Amazon' Kettle. The goal of this 46-hour hackathon was to come up with and build a terrible idea. Ours was a kettle which requires you to listen to a full ad, without skipping, in order to use it.


Block Generator

A program which spawns different types of blocks with different travel paths, created as an Object-Oriented Programming exercise in my CS230 (Object-Oriented Software Development) course.


Summer Lab

I took part in Summer Lab 2024, which was a four-week program dedicated to identifying a problem, validating that problem, and developing a pitch for our solution. My team’s final showcase of our project, Project Kaiãrahi, took the form of a pitch presentation, for which I spoke and developed a Figma prototype based on our research.
